The Timeline

From Portsmouth to
the Hall of Fame

Every milestone in the life of the greatest undefeated quarterback in football history. From a small Ohio river town in 1950 to the College Football Hall of Fame in 2022 — and beyond.

The Journey

A Life Undefeated

Twenty defining moments across seven decades. From Portsmouth, Ohio to the highest halls of football history.

1950

Born in Portsmouth, Ohio

Charles “Chuck” Ealey born January 6, 1950 in Portsmouth, Ohio — a small working-class city on the banks of the Ohio River where a future legend would take his first breath.

1964

Enters Notre Dame High School

Begins playing football under Hall of Fame coach Ed Miller, who recognized not just the arm and the legs, but the leadership that would define Chuck’s entire career.

1967

Ohio State Championship

Completes an undefeated 18-0 record at Notre Dame High School, wins the Ohio state football title, and also wins the basketball championship. The pattern of winning was already set.

1968

Scholarship to Toledo

Receives a football scholarship to the University of Toledo, beginning the next chapter of a career that would rewrite the record books.

1969

First Undefeated Season

Leads the Toledo Rockets to a perfect season and the program’s first Mid-American Conference championship. The Rockets are unstoppable with Ealey under center.

1969

First Tangerine Bowl MVP

Toledo defeats Davidson 56–33 in the Tangerine Bowl. Chuck Ealey is named Most Valuable Player — the first of three consecutive bowl MVPs.

1970

Second Perfect Season

Another undefeated campaign, another MAC title, and a second consecutive Tangerine Bowl MVP performance as Toledo defeats William & Mary 40–12.

1971

All-American Season

Third undefeated year. Named First Team All-American. Finishes 8th in Heisman Trophy voting — the first MAC player in history to receive Heisman votes. Third consecutive Tangerine Bowl MVP as Toledo defeats Richmond 28–3. The record stands at a perfect 35-0.

1972

The NFL Draft Snub

All 26 NFL teams pass on Chuck Ealey across 17 rounds and 442 picks. The greatest winning quarterback in college football history is completely ignored. Chuck refuses to play anything other than quarterback and signs with the Hamilton Tiger-Cats of the CFL.

1972

Grey Cup Champion

Leads the Hamilton Tiger-Cats to a Grey Cup victory over Saskatchewan, 13–10. Completes 18 of 29 passes for 291 yards and 1 touchdown, adds 63 rushing yards, and engineers a game-winning 68-yard drive in the final two minutes. Becomes the first Black quarterback to win the Grey Cup. Named Grey Cup MVP and CFL Rookie of the Year.

1973

Dominant Second Season

Delivers a 58.6% completion rate, 2,312 passing yards, and 687 rushing yards in his second CFL season, cementing his status as one of the league’s elite dual-threat quarterbacks.

1974

Traded to Winnipeg

Moves to the Winnipeg Blue Bombers mid-season, bringing his talent and leadership to a new franchise in western Canada.

1975

Joins Toronto Argonauts

Traded to the Toronto Argonauts, beginning a three-season stint with the storied franchise in the city that would become his permanent home.

1978

Career Ends

Suffers a collapsed lung, forcing an end to his CFL career. Over 7 professional seasons, Chuck compiled 13,326 passing yards, 82 passing touchdowns, and 3,984 rushing yards — a complete dual-threat quarterback to the very end.

1978

Toledo Hall of Fame

Inducted into the University of Toledo Athletic Hall of Fame, the first of four Halls of Fame that would honor his extraordinary career.

1988

MAC Hall of Fame

Inducted as a charter member of the Mid-American Conference Hall of Fame — a fitting tribute to the quarterback who put the MAC on the national map.

2006

Founds the Chuck Ealey Foundation

Creates the Undefeated Spirit Award and establishes scholarship programs to invest in the next generation of leaders, carrying the lessons of 35-0 far beyond the football field.

2008

PBS Documentary

Undefeated: The Chuck Ealey Story airs on PBS on November 27, bringing his remarkable journey of triumph and injustice to a national television audience.

2022

College Football Hall of Fame

Inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame as part of the Class of 2022. More than fifty years after going 35-0, the football establishment finally gave Chuck Ealey the recognition he had always deserved.

2024

National High School Football Hall of Fame

Inducted into the National High School Football Hall of Fame, completing the full circle from Portsmouth to national recognition. Four Halls of Fame. Zero defeats. The Undefeated Spirit lives on.

“Winning is not about the score on the scoreboard. It’s about the attitude that you don’t quit, that you don’t give up, no matter what life throws at you.”

— Chuck Ealey

The Undefeated Spirit Continues

Chuck Ealey’s timeline is still being written. Through the Foundation, through speaking, and through the lives he touches every day, the legacy grows. Be part of the next chapter.
