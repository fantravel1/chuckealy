Frameworks

Mental Models for Builders

Thinking tools forged from decades of building, breaking, and rebuilding. These are the lenses through which every decision gets made.

Decision Framework

The Receipts Framework

Every claim needs evidence. Every outcome needs measurement. The Receipts Framework is a discipline of proving what you ship, documenting what you build, and letting results speak louder than narratives. No vanity metrics. No hand-waving. Receipts or it did not happen.

Operations Model

The 3-Layer Operations Model

Layer one: infrastructure that does not break. Layer two: processes that scale without adding headcount. Layer three: intelligence that turns data into decisions. Most teams only build layer one and wonder why they cannot scale. The model forces clarity about what is missing.

Execution Principle

Ship > Perfect

Perfection is procrastination wearing a lab coat. The fastest path to a great product is shipping a good one, measuring what actually happens, and iterating with real data instead of assumptions. Velocity compounds. Hesitation decays. Ship it, learn from it, improve it.

Team Architecture

The Ownership Radius

Every person on a team should own a clearly defined radius of responsibility with full authority to act within it. Ambiguity kills velocity. When ownership is clear, decisions happen fast, accountability is natural, and the team operates like a system instead of a committee.
