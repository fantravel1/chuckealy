Growth

Turning a Stalled Pipeline Into a $12M Engine

The Problem: A mid-market SaaS company had flat revenue for three consecutive quarters. The sales pipeline was unstructured, the CRM was a graveyard of stale leads, and the team had no repeatable process. Marketing and sales operated in silos with zero shared accountability.

The Action: Audited the entire revenue operation from lead generation to close. Rebuilt the CRM architecture, implemented a structured lead-scoring model, and designed a multi-stage pipeline with clear ownership at every step. Hired and onboarded six new account executives and two SDRs. Created joint marketing-sales playbooks and weekly pipeline reviews.

The Measurable Result: $12M in new annual recurring revenue within 18 months. Pipeline coverage ratio improved from 1.2x to 4.1x. Average deal velocity decreased by 35%.

Team Building

Building a 60-Person Organization From Zero

The Problem: A newly funded startup had capital but no organizational structure. The founders were doing everything themselves, from hiring to product decisions to customer support. There was no org chart, no hiring pipeline, no onboarding process, and no cultural framework. Growth was stalling because the founders were the bottleneck.

The Action: Designed the entire organizational architecture from scratch: department structure, reporting lines, role definitions, and compensation bands. Built a hiring pipeline that sourced from over 2,000 candidates. Created a structured onboarding program with 30/60/90-day milestones. Established a cultural operating system centered on autonomy, clarity, and accountability.

The Measurable Result: 60 hires across engineering, product, sales, and operations within 24 months. 94% retention of key talent through a hypergrowth phase. Employee NPS scores averaged 72.

Operations

Reducing Operational Costs by 40% Without Layoffs

The Problem: A 150-person company was burning through cash at an unsustainable rate. Operational expenses had ballooned due to redundant vendor contracts, manual workflows consuming thousands of labor hours, and a sprawling tech stack with overlapping tools. The board wanted 30% cuts; the default plan was layoffs.

The Action: Conducted a full operational audit across every department. Mapped all vendor contracts and renegotiated the top 15 by spend. Identified 12 manual processes ripe for automation and deployed workflow tools that eliminated over 4,000 hours of annual labor. Consolidated the tech stack from 47 tools to 22 without losing capability.

The Measurable Result: $3.2M in annual savings — a 40% reduction in operational costs. Zero layoffs. Employee satisfaction scores increased by 18 points because teams were freed from tedious manual work.

$3.2M saved/year 12 months Head of Operations — Lead

Technology

Migrating a Legacy Platform to Cloud in 6 Months

The Problem: A financial services firm was running mission-critical applications on aging on-premise infrastructure with 99.2% uptime — well below industry standards. Deployments took two weeks, the monolithic architecture made feature iteration painfully slow, and the team lived in constant fear of catastrophic failure during peak periods.

The Action: Designed a phased cloud migration strategy that prioritized the highest-risk, highest-value services first. Led the decomposition of the monolith into 14 microservices. Implemented CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure-as-code, and automated testing. Orchestrated parallel running of old and new systems for zero-downtime cutover. Trained the entire engineering team on cloud-native practices.

The Measurable Result: Uptime improved from 99.2% to 99.97%. Deployment frequency went from biweekly to multiple times per day. Infrastructure costs dropped 28% despite handling 3x more traffic. The migration completed two weeks ahead of schedule.

99.97% uptime 6 months VP of Engineering — Migration Lead

Strategy

Pivoting a Failing Product Into a Market Leader

The Problem: A B2B software product had been in market for 18 months with declining user engagement and a churn rate of 11% monthly. The original product-market fit thesis was wrong. The sales team was demoralized, the product roadmap was bloated with features nobody used, and the company had eight months of runway remaining.

The Action: Led a rapid customer discovery sprint — 40 interviews in two weeks. Identified a high-value adjacent use case that existing users were hacking together. Stripped the product roadmap to six core features. Repositioned the brand and messaging around the new use case. Restructured pricing from per-seat to usage-based. Executed a targeted go-to-market campaign to the new ICP.

The Measurable Result: Monthly churn dropped from 11% to 2.8%. MRR grew 340% within nine months. The company secured a Series A at a $28M valuation — up from a $4M pre-pivot valuation.

340% MRR growth 9 months Interim CEO — Full P&L Owner

Operations

Tripling Engineering Output Without Adding Headcount

The Problem: A 30-person engineering team was shipping at a fraction of its potential. Sprint completion rates hovered around 40%. Technical debt was accumulating faster than it was being paid down. Dependencies between teams created constant blockers, and there was no shared definition of "done." The CTO had resigned, and morale was at an all-time low.

The Action: Reorganized the team from a functional structure into cross-functional pods, each owning a full vertical slice of the product. Introduced clear sprint contracts with measurable acceptance criteria. Established a weekly tech-debt budget (20% of sprint capacity). Implemented async standups, blameless retrospectives, and a transparent shipping dashboard visible to the entire company.

The Measurable Result: Sprint completion rate rose from 40% to 91%. Feature throughput tripled within four months. Critical bugs in production dropped by 65%. Engineering NPS increased from 22 to 68.
