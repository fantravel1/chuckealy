Grey Cup Most Valuable Player
Named game’s outstanding player

Passed over by all 26 NFL teams across 17 rounds and 442 picks in the 1972 draft, Chuck Ealey signed with the Hamilton Tiger-Cats of the Canadian Football League. In his very first professional season, he led the team to the Grey Cup — Canada’s Super Bowl equivalent.

Trailing in the fourth quarter, Ealey engineered a 68-yard game-winning drive in the final two minutes. He completed 18 of 29 passes for 291 yards and added 63 yards on the ground. He was named the Grey Cup’s Most Valuable Player — as a rookie. It was the ultimate answer to the NFL’s silence.
